M.Sc. Dissertation Project

Fabrication & Characterization of Bio-Based Piezoelectric Thin Films Using Aloe-Vera

Department of Physics | M.Sc. Final Year Dissertation

This work presents a study focusing on exploring the extraction, structural characterisation, and advanced applications of Aloe vera, highlighting its versatility as a natural bio-organic material for energy-harvesting technologies. First, the study investigates optimised extraction methods to isolate active components while preserving their structural integrity. High-speed centrifugation at 6,000 rpm at 10°C was identified as the optimal condition for maximising gel recovery and quality. Additionally, ultrasonic-assisted extraction significantly enhanced the recovery and diffusion kinetics of the bioactive compound aloin compared to traditional batch methods. This work pioneers the application of Aloe vera in sustainable electronics. Processed Aloe vera dry films exhibit room-temperature piezoelectricity, driven by the long-range ordering and hydrogen bonding of acemannan chains. These pure Aloe vera films were successfully deployed in piezoelectric energy harvesters to convert biomechanical energy into electrical power, successfully generating a peak-to-peak voltage of 600 mV (+300 mV and -300 mV). Making it highly effective for eco-friendly, flexible pressure sensors. This research demonstrates that Aloe vera is a sustainable resource capable of driving self-powered biocompatible electronics.
