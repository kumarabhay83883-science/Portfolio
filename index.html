M.Sc. Dissertation Project

Fabrication & Characterization of Bio-Based Piezoelectric Thin Films Using Aloe-Vera

Department of Physics | M.Sc. Final Year Dissertation

Synthesis and characterization of sustainable, bio-compatible piezoelectric thin films utilizing Aloe Vera and nanocellulose composites. The experimental workflow involves drop-casting, slow ambient drying, controlled muffle furnace heat treatment, and contact poling for electromechanical response.

Fabricated Bio-Thin Film Sample
Voltage Output / Characterization Graph
  • Synthesis Workflow: Drop-casting & ambient drying of Aloe Vera/nanocellulose matrix.
  • Thermal Treatment: Muffle furnace sintering for crystalline alignment.
  • Electrical Poling: High-voltage contact poling to induce dipole orientation.
